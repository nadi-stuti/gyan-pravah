# Diwali - Quiz Questions

**Category:** Utsav (Festivals)  
**Total Questions:** 30  
**Difficulty Distribution:** 12 Easy, 12 Medium, 6 Hard

---

## Easy Questions (1-12)

### Question 1
**Q:** Diwali celebrates return of?  
**A)** Krishna  
**B)** Rama  
**C)** Ganesha  
**D)** Hanuman  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Diwali celebrates the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after 14 years of exile.

### Question 2
**Q:** Diwali lasts for how many days?  
**A)** 3  
**B)** 4  
**C)** 5  
**D)** 7  
**Correct Answer:** C  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Diwali is celebrated for five days from Dhanteras to Bhai Dooj.

### Question 3
**Q:** Diwali is festival of?  
**A)** Colors  
**B)** Lights  
**C)** Water  
**D)** Fire  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Diwali is called the Festival of Lights.

### Question 4
**Q:** Which goddess is worshipped on Diwali?  
**A)** Saraswati  
**B)** Lakshmi  
**C)** Durga  
**D)** Kali  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Goddess Lakshmi, the deity of wealth and prosperity, is worshipped on Diwali.

### Question 5
**Q:** Diwali falls in which month?  
**A)** Kartik  
**B)** Ashwin  
**C)** Margashirsha  
**D)** Chaitra  
**Correct Answer:** A  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Diwali falls on Amavasya (new moon) of Kartik month.

### Question 6
**Q:** What is lit on Diwali?  
**A)** Candles  
**B)** Diyas  
**C)** Fireworks  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Diyas (lamps), candles, and fireworks are all lit during Diwali.

### Question 7
**Q:** Rangoli is made with?  
**A)** Colors  
**B)** Flowers  
**C)** Rice flour  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Rangoli can be made with colored powders, flowers, or rice flour.

### Question 8
**Q:** Which demon was killed by Krishna on Diwali?  
**A)** Ravana  
**B)** Narakasura  
**C)** Mahishasura  
**D)** Hiranyakashipu  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** In South India, Diwali celebrates Krishna's victory over demon Narakasura.

### Question 9
**Q:** Diwali signifies victory of?  
**A)** Light over darkness  
**B)** Good over evil  
**C)** Knowledge over ignorance  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Diwali symbolizes the victory of light, good, and knowledge.

### Question 10
**Q:** First day of Diwali is?  
**A)** Dhanteras  
**B)** Choti Diwali  
**C)** Lakshmi Puja  
**D)** Govardhan Puja  
**Correct Answer:** A  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Dhanteras is the first day of the five-day Diwali festival.

### Question 11
**Q:** Traditional sweet eaten on Diwali?  
**A)** Ladoo  
**B)** Barfi  
**C)** Gulab Jamun  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Various sweets like ladoo, barfi, and gulab jamun are prepared for Diwali.

### Question 12
**Q:** Diwali is also called?  
**A)** Deepavali  
**B)** Festival of Lights  
**C)** Both Deepavali & Festival of Lights  
**D)** Neither  
**Correct Answer:** C  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Diwali is also known as Deepavali, meaning "row of lights."

## Medium Questions (13-24)

### Question 13
**Q:** Lakshmi Puja is on which day?  
**A)** First  
**B)** Second  
**C)** Third  
**D)** Fourth  
**Correct Answer:** C  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Lakshmi Puja is performed on the third day, the main Diwali day.

### Question 14
**Q:** Dhanteras celebrates?  
**A)** Wealth  
**B)** Health  
**C)** Dhanvantari  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Dhanteras honors Dhanvantari (god of health) and celebrates wealth.

### Question 15
**Q:** Diwali date changes because of?  
**A)** Solar calendar  
**B)** Lunar calendar  
**C)** Both  
**D)** Neither  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Diwali follows the lunar calendar, so its date varies each year.

### Question 16
**Q:** In which epic is Diwali story mentioned?  
**A)** Ramayana  
**B)** Mahabharata  
**C)** Bhagavata  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Different aspects of Diwali are mentioned in various epics and Puranas.

### Question 17
**Q:** Govardhan Puja is associated with?  
**A)** Rama  
**B)** Krishna  
**C)** Shiva  
**D)** Ganesha  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Govardhan Puja celebrates Krishna lifting Govardhan mountain.

### Question 18
**Q:** Bhai Dooj celebrates relationship between?  
**A)** Husband-wife  
**B)** Brother-sister  
**C)** Father-son  
**D)** Friends  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Bhai Dooj celebrates the bond between brothers and sisters.

### Question 19
**Q:** Naraka Chaturdashi is also called?  
**A)** Choti Diwali  
**B)** Kali Chaudas  
**C)** Roop Chaudas  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** The second day has various names in different regions.

### Question 20
**Q:** In Jainism, Diwali marks?  
**A)** Mahavira's birth  
**B)** Mahavira's nirvana  
**C)** New year  
**D)** Nothing  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** For Jains, Diwali marks Lord Mahavira's attainment of nirvana.

### Question 21
**Q:** In Sikhism, Diwali celebrates?  
**A)** Guru Nanak's birth  
**B)** Bandi Chhor Divas  
**C)** Vaisakhi  
**D)** Nothing  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Sikhs celebrate Bandi Chhor Divas (release of Guru Hargobind) during Diwali.

### Question 22
**Q:** What metal is bought on Dhanteras?  
**A)** Gold  
**B)** Silver  
**C)** Copper  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Buying gold, silver, or other metals is considered auspicious on Dhanteras.

### Question 23
**Q:** Annakut is offering of?  
**A)** Sweets  
**B)** 56 dishes  
**C)** Mountain of food  
**D)** Fruits  
**Correct Answer:** C  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Annakut means "mountain of food" offered to Krishna on Govardhan Puja.

### Question 24
**Q:** Diwali cleaning represents?  
**A)** Hygiene  
**B)** Welcoming Lakshmi  
**C)** Removing negativity  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Explanation:** Pre-Diwali cleaning has practical, spiritual, and symbolic significance.

## Hard Questions (25-30)

### Question 25
**Q:** Deepavali word comes from which language?  
**A)** Sanskrit  
**B)** Hindi  
**C)** Tamil  
**D)** Telugu  
**Correct Answer:** A  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** "Deepavali" is a Sanskrit word meaning "row of lamps."

### Question 26
**Q:** Diwali is celebrated in how many countries officially?  
**A)** 5  
**B)** 10  
**C)** 15+  
**D)** Only India  
**Correct Answer:** C  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** Diwali is an official holiday in over 15 countries including India, Nepal, Fiji, etc.

### Question 27
**Q:** Historical year when Rama returned to Ayodhya?  
**A)** 5114 BCE  
**B)** 7000 BCE  
**C)** Various estimates  
**D)** 1000 BCE  
**Correct Answer:** C  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** Various Hindu calendars give different historical dates for Rama's return.

### Question 28
**Q:** The practice of gambling on Diwali relates to?  
**A)** Shiva-Parvati  
**B)** Lakshmi worship  
**C)** Ancient tradition  
**D)** All options  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** Gambling tradition has connections to Shiva-Parvati lore and wealth symbolism.

### Question 29
**Q:** Economic impact of Diwali in India?  
**A)** Minimal  
**B)** Moderate  
**C)** Highest sales season  
**D)** Negative  
**Correct Answer:** C  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** Diwali is India's biggest shopping and sales season, like Christmas in West.

### Question 30
**Q:** Earliest mention of Diwali celebration in?  
**A)** Vedas  
**B)** Puranas  
**C)** Skanda Purana  
**D)** Mahabharata  
**Correct Answer:** C  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** The Skanda Purana contains early references to Diwali celebrations.
