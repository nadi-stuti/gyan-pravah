## Easy Questions (1-12)

### Question 1
**Q:** Kashi is located in which state?  
**A)** Bihar  
**B)** Uttar Pradesh  
**C)** Madhya Pradesh  
**D)** Rajasthan  
**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Kashi (Varanasi) is located in Uttar Pradesh on the banks of river Ganga.

**A)** Yamuna  
**B)** Ganga  
**C)** Saraswati  
**D)** Narmada

**Difficulty:** Easy  
**Explanation:** Kashi is situated on the sacred banks of river Ganga (Ganges).

## Hard Questions (25-30)

**Correct Answer:** B  
**Difficulty:** Hard

### Question 26

**A)** Self-manifested  
**B)** Installed by Shiva  
**C)** Brought from Kailash  
**D)** Created by sages  
**Correct Answer:** A  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** The original Vishwanath jyotirlinga is believed to be swayambhu (self-manifested).

### Question 27
**Q:** Kashi's cosmic significance in Hindu cosmology?  
**A)** Center of universe  
**B)** Shiva's trident tip  
**C)** Never destroyed in pralaya  
**D)** All above  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** Kashi is considered the cosmic center, tip of Shiva's trident, and eternally protected from cosmic dissolution.

### Question 28
**Q:** Kashi's Vedic name "Kashi" means?  
**A)** Luminous  
**B)** Shining  
**C)** City of light  
**D)** All above  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** The name "Kashi" derives from the root "kash" meaning to shine or be luminous.

### Question 29
**Q:** Kashi's influence on Indian philosophy through?  
**A)** Advaita Vedanta  
**B)** Shaiva philosophy  
**C)** Sanskrit learning  
**D)** All above  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** Kashi has been a major center for Advaita Vedanta, Shaiva philosophy, and Sanskrit scholarship.

### Question 30
**Q:** Kashi's unique spiritual concept of "Kashi Labh"?  
**A)** Automatic liberation  
**B)** Shiva's whispered mantra  
**C)** Death in sacred geography  
**D)** All above  
**Correct Answer:** D  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Explanation:** "Kashi Labh" refers to the belief that Shiva whispers the taraka mantra to those who die in Kashi, granting liberation.
